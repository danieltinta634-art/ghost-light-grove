# Cliff — Character Deep Dive

> Townsperson (not a marriage candidate). The town's gravedigger & grounds keeper.

## The Basics (established canon)
- **Species:** Zombie — the town's only one. A mindless risen, found by **Myra**, uplifted to sentience by **Victor**. Like all uplifts: **no memories of his past life, only muscle memory.**
- **Role:** Grave digger & grounds keeper.
- **Home:** A hut by the **graveyard at the base of the manor cliff-stairs** (between the manor and town — the player passes it daily).
- **Appearance:** Green skin, white hair, pale-yellow eyes, skinny build, no shoes. **A tattered hat with a small flower tucked in it** (rhymes with the flowers on his chest — the tender caretaker who likes to look nice).
- **Pet:** **Doug**, a crow who constantly pecks at Cliff's corpse-flesh. Cliff doesn't mind.
- **Quirks:** Sleeps in a freshly-dug grave (shovel at the foot, headboard reading "Here Lies Cliff"). Pins flowers to his chest at events so he "smells nice." Possibly calls his shovel a lady's name.

## Emotional Core (deep dive)
- **He is at peace.** Genuinely, gently content. He doesn't mourn the past he can't remember — he has a job, a crow, a home among the graves, and that is enough. His contentment is quietly profound: the dead man who found happiness in a small, useful life.
- **The melancholy is the player's, not his.** We feel the weight of his blank past; Cliff serenely does not. That gap is the whole emotional engine of the character — never have him angst about it.

## The Muscle Memory — his defining thread
- His hands remember **loving someone.** He tends **every grave** as if it holds someone precious: immaculate upkeep, fresh flowers laid just so, headstones brushed clean, soft words murmured to them. He has no idea why — *it just feels right.*
- Care is the one thing that survived his death. His mind is a blank; his hands are devoted.
- Implies (never confirmed) that in life he cared for people — a father, a nurse, a devoted partner. **Left deliberately unconfirmed** (matches his "at peace / not searching" stance — neither he nor the game needs to solve it).

## Thematic Resonance
- **Cliff and the reaper (player) are complements.** Cliff tends the *resting places* of the dead (bodies, graves); the player-reaper sees the *souls* on. The keeper of homes and the guide of spirits — two halves of caring for the dead. Rich material once reaper gameplay unlocks.

## The Graveyard (canon)
- Cliff buries and tends **the mindless risen who were never saved** — the ones who rose but were never uplifted into people like he was. He cares for the graves of his own kind, the unlucky ones.
- **The tragedy he'll never know:** Cliff was almost one of them. He rose mindless too; Myra found him and Victor uplifted him instead of leaving him in the ground he now tends. He cares for the exact fate he escaped — with flowers, soft words, and no idea why it matters so much.
- **Systems loop:** the player clears wild risen (bodies) in the Deep Woods and reaps spirits (souls) in the caves; **Cliff is who lays the bodies to rest.** Every combat/reaper system feeds his graveyard. (Possible gameplay: deliver cleared remains to Cliff for burial.)
