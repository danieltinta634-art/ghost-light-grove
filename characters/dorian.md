Dorian is a male siren with **slight water-like features** — and, being a siren,
he is **extremely good-looking**, the supernatural, slightly-unfair kind of
attractive that comes with the territory. He **runs the lighthouse** on the coast
and **plays Benny's bar some nights.** ✅ His nature is song; soulful, a touch
melancholy — the dreamer/artist of the cast. (Name: "of the sea" + the Dorian
musical mode.)

❓ **Emotional core — TBD.** Open thread, not locked. One option on the table
(the siren subversion): being effortlessly adored by everyone means he can never
tell who's with him by *choice* vs. the allure — a loneliness underneath the good
looks. Available if you want it for the romance arc; otherwise his interior is
still to be defined.

🔶 **The lighthouse (proposed):** a keeper of light in a world of permanent night.
His beam — and his voice — reach out across the dark water and **guide the lost
safely in.** The siren whose song leads travellers to *safety* instead of doom:
the same subversion the whole Grove runs on. (Hook: this may be how **Marshal**
found his way up the river system to the Grove — he followed the light, or the
song.)

🔶 **Why the Grove drew him (proposed):** outside the veil, a siren is a hazard —
feared, exiled, blamed for a song he can't fully switch off. The Grove is the one
place he can exist without doing harm, among others the world also called
monsters. He turned the lure outward and downward, into a lighthouse.

🔶 **Romance arc (proposed, IF the core above is adopted):** the player's
affection has to prove it's *real and chosen*, not just the allure talking — and
the payoff is being the one person he believes actually chose him. (Only works if
you take the "adored by all, sure of none" angle; otherwise his arc is open.)
